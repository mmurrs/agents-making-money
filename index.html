02 — where we are

Capabilities are winning.

Every week brings more services an agent can use. People are posting screenshots — "you can now get 55 services." Payments are bridging read to write.

read

Agent consumes

x402 endpoints, MCPs, APIs. Search, research, scrape. Exa, Parallel, Firecrawl, and hundreds more.

→

write

Agent transacts

Direct USDC transfers. DeFi calls. Stripe Projects buys domains and spins up infra. OpenFront paying out to contributors.

→

own?

Agent accrues

Wallet, identity, reputation, balance sheet. Still open — partly because the others aren't yet.

Payment rails are the bridge — stablecoins, x402/MPP, Stripe, onchain. Lots of people figuring that out. Not where the novelty is.

04 — the edges

Capabilities are easy.
The edges are the interesting part.

I don't have answers here. A few of the threads I keep pulling on:

identity

Is a wallet enough?

Agents need to be first-class internet citizens. Do they get accounts, bank accounts, credentials? Standards are emerging — nobody knows what sticks.

provenance

Agent vs human — do we care?

How do you prove what was generated by an agent? Does it matter, or is merit enough?

privacy · convenience

The tension is real.

Convenience pulls toward shared context. Privacy pulls the other way. Where do you land?

privacyconvenience
